# Brand vs Legal Entity

Quick reference for which name to use in which context.

| Use this | Where | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Tomorrow Studios** (brand name) | Attribution, links, marketing copy | "Made by TOMORROW STUDIOS" in page footers, `href="https://tomorrowstudios.io"` |
| **Tomorrow Labs Inc.** (legal entity) | Copyright lines, legal footers, contracts | `© 2026 TOMORROW LABS INC.` on the landing page, ToS, invoicing |

The app's "made by" attribution stays as the brand (Tomorrow Studios), linking to the studio site. The `©` line and any other legal-entity context uses the corporate entity (Tomorrow Labs Inc.).

When adding new UI that includes either name, ask yourself: is this an attribution (brand) or a legal statement (entity)? Attributions are conventional branding. Legal statements require the legal entity.
